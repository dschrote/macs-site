Governance

This page defines how MACS is maintained: responsibilities, decision and publication logic, versioning, and the separation between Core and Annexes. The objective is stability, traceability, and citeability.

The current status and binding versions are shown on the Status page.

Normative terms

MACS uses the following terms in a normative way:

  • must: required for MACS conformance
  • should: recommended; deviations should be justified and documented
  • may: optional

Role of the editor

The Minimum Acceptable Crypto Standard (MACS) is maintained by an editor.

The editor is responsible for:

  • coherence of the standard,
  • versioning and publication of binding versions,
  • preserving the integrity of MACS.

Binding versions of MACS are published and versioned exclusively by the editor. Other versions, derivatives or paraphrases do not constitute MACS conformance. No ownership or exclusivity claims are asserted.

Nature of the standard

MACS is an open reference standard. It is designed to be:

  • technology- and asset-neutral,
  • free from privileging specific products, vendors or implementations,
  • applicable independent of narratives or interest positions.

MACS defines minimum requirements for governance and accountability, not broader best-practice or optimisation criteria.

Versioning and stability

MACS Core follows a strict versioning logic:

  • Core versions are frozen and form the binding reference framework.
  • Changes to the Core occur only when materially required and result in a new major version.
  • Clarifications and thematic additions are provided exclusively via annexes.

The current binding version is published on this website.

Annexes

Annexes are intended for:

  • thematic clarification of specific aspects,
  • context for specific use cases,
  • awareness of potential regulatory, organisational or technological impacts without conclusively regulating or assessing them.

Annexes are not part of the MACS Core and do not change its scope of validity.

Scope and limitations

MACS is not:

  • legal, tax or audit advice,
  • a certification or quality statement,
  • a guarantee of regulatory or economic outcomes.

Applying MACS is the responsibility of the respective organisation.

Further development

MACS is developed:

  • transparently,
  • versioned,
  • with changes documented in a traceable manner.

Proposals, comments or discussion contributions may be considered but do not create any entitlement to change or inclusion.

Policy-ready statements

  • Binding versions of MACS must be versioned and published on this website.
  • The MACS Core should remain stable; content changes should be exceptional.
  • Annexes may provide clarifications and thematic additions without changing the scope of the Core.
